Book Recommendations

Gone Girl

by Gillian Flynn

On the couple’s 5th wedding anniversary, Amy disappears and Nick becomes the prime suspect. A twisted portrait of marriage, identity and deceit.

⭐ 4.1 / 5

And Then There Were None

by Agatha Christie

Ten strangers are invited to a secluded island under different pretexts. Upon arrival, they discover their mysterious host is missing and each guest is accused of a past crime. One by one, they start dying in ways eerily similar to a children’s nursery rhyme. With no way off the island and no one they can trust, paranoia grows as they realize the murderer must be among them.

⭐ 4.3 / 5

The Only One Left

by Riley Sager

A caregiver is assigned to an old mansion to look after a woman accused of murdering her family decades ago. As she learns more about the past, eerie clues suggest the real killer might still be alive — and closer than she thinks.

⭐ 4.13 / 5

Those Empty Eyes

by Charlie Donlea

After surviving the brutal murder of her family, Alex Armstrong is wrongly accused and forced to rebuild her life under a new identity. Years later, while investigating a missing person case, she uncovers shocking links to her own past and the real killer.

⭐ 4.15 / 5

The Kite Runner

by Khaled Hosseini

Set in Afghanistan, the story follows Amir, a privileged boy, and Hassan, his loyal friend and servant’s son. After a heartbreaking betrayal, Amir’s guilt haunts him into adulthood, driving him on a journey of redemption amid his country’s turbulent history.

⭐ 4.35 / 5

As Long as the Lemon Trees Grow

by Zoulfa Katouh

During the Syrian revolution, Salama, a young pharmacy student turned volunteer, struggles between staying to help her war-torn country or fleeing for safety. Haunted by trauma and loss, she must find courage and hope amid devastation and love.

⭐ 4.46 / 5

Laut Bercerita

by Leila S. Chudori

Set in Indonesia during the late 1990s, the novel follows Biru Laut, a student activist who fights against an oppressive regime. Through his story and the grief of those left behind, it portrays the pain of loss, the cruelty of enforced disappearances, and the enduring hope for justice.

⭐ 4.62 / 5

Di Tanah Lada

The Little Prince

by Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

A young prince travels from planet to planet, meeting curious adults and learning lessons about love, friendship, and the essence of what truly matters in life.

⭐ 4.33 / 5

Matilda

by Roald Dahl

Matilda, a brilliant and kind-hearted girl, uses her extraordinary intelligence and telekinetic powers to overcome neglectful parents and a cruel school headmistress, discovering the power of courage and self-belief.

⭐ 4.33 / 5

The Nightingale

by Kristin Hannah

During World War II in Nazi-occupied France, two sisters fight to survive and resist in their own ways. One through quiet endurance, the other by joining the dangerous French Resistance. Revealing courage, sacrifice, and love.

⭐ 4.64 / 5

Gone With the Wind

by Margaret Mitchell

Set during the American Civil War and Reconstruction, Scarlett O’Hara, a strong-willed Southern woman, struggles to survive, protect her family, and navigate love and loss amid the collapse of her world.

⭐ 4.31 / 5

All the Light We Cannot See

by Anthony Doerr

During World War II, blind French girl Marie-Laure and German boy Werner live vastly different lives that eventually intersect amid the chaos of war, revealing resilience, hope, and the unseen connections that bind people.

⭐ 4.31 / 5

Murder on the Orient Express

by Agatha Christie

Just after midnight, a snowdrift stops the famous Orient Express in its tracks as it travels through the mountainous Balkans. The luxurious train is surprisingly full for the time of the year but, by the morning, it is one passenger fewer. An American tycoon lies dead in his compartment, stabbed a dozen times, his door locked from the inside.

⭐ 4.20 / 5

The Hound of the Baskervilles

by Arthur Conan Doyle

In this, one of the most famous of Doyle's mysteries, the tale of an ancient curse and a savage ghostly hound comes frighteningly to life. The gray towers of Baskerville Hall and the wild open country of Dartmoor will haunt the reader as Holmes and Watson seek to unravel the many secrets of the misty English bogs.

⭐ 4.14 / 5

Why You Should Read More Fiction

Fiction helps us understand emotions, motives, and complex human behaviors. By immersing yourself in stories, you train your mind to empathize and imagine new perspectives and skills that are vital in life.
